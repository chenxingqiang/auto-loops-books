\chapter{Attention Online Softmax Optimization}
\label{chap:ch10}
\typeout{START_CHAPTER "ch10" \theabspage}

Can software alone recover decode when interconnect and HBM budgets are tight? Fregly's DeepSeek case study shows that mechanical sympathy---algorithms written for the memory hierarchy they run on---can match frontier quality on constrained H800 clusters \citep{liu2024deepseekv3,patel2025llminference}. The same principle applies at batch-1 decode inside a single layer: naive attention materializes an $S\times S$ softmax map, and even a streaming $1\times T$ score row becomes catastrophic when fused boundaries force write/read pairs between KV blocks \citep{dao2022flashattention,dao2023flashdecoding,memoryfloor2026}.

\textbf{Online softmax} is the attention-side embodiment of that sympathy: instead of storing $P=\mathrm{softmax}(QK^\top)$, the kernel maintains running statistics $(m,\ell)$ (row max and sum of exponentials) and a partial value accumulator $o$, updating all three as each KV block arrives \citep{dao2022flashattention}. FlashAttention made IO-aware tiling mainstream; Flash-Decoding parallelizes the KV dimension while preserving online state \citep{dao2023flashdecoding}. This chapter explains the numerics, residency tables on GPU/XDNA/CPU, and the \textbf{fusion boundaries} where online carriers must hand off to $PV$ and Chapter~11 MegaKernels without touching HBM \citep{taxbreak2026,yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}.

The common production mistake is treating softmax as a cheap epilogue after a GEMM. At batch-1 decode, softmax is not one kernel---it is a \emph{state machine} embedded inside the KV loop \citep{kwon2023vllm,patel2025llminference}. TaxBreak dense Llama-3.2-1B averages 847.5 kernel launches per output token on H100; online softmax alone will not collapse that count---fusion with QKV and $PV$ inside a MegaKernel will (Chapter~11) \citep{taxbreak2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs}. But MegaKernels \emph{require} online carriers to stay in registers or shared memory across the attention--MLP seam \citep{semianalysis2024tma,amd2024aie}. Spill $(m,\ell,o)$ between blocks and you pay full-width traffic every context step while launches/token flatline \citep{memoryfloor2026}.

\paragraph{Decode vs prefill softmax shape.} Prefill processes many query rows at once; decode adds \emph{one} new query row against entire KV history \citep{dao2023flashdecoding,kwon2023vllm}. Online softmax in decode is a loop over KV \emph{blocks}, not a single matmul epilogue \citep{dao2022flashattention}. Benchmarks that only tune prefill softmax miss the carrier residency story that determines whether Chapter~11 fusion is even legal \citep{taxbreak2026,yirage2026}.


\paragraph{Training vs inference scope.} Online softmax also accelerates training attention for long sequences---FlashAttention\'s original motivation \citep{dao2022flashattention}. This chapter emphasizes decode because batch-1 residency and MegaKernel fusion are decode-specific engineering pressures \citep{taxbreak2026,dao2023flashdecoding,patel2025llminference}. Training chapters in other books cover overlapping comm/compute; our decode compiler story stands alone \citep{dlbook,chen2020compilerbasedhardw,yirage2026}.
\paragraph{Goodput for attention.} Raw FLOPs on $QK^\top$ mislead: useful decode work is the final attention output bytes delivered per ms/token, discounting materialized $P$, redundant HBM round trips, and extra launches \citep{patel2025llminference,memoryfloor2026}. Online softmax raises attention goodput by keeping exponentials and partial $PV$ on-chip---the attention analog of Meta's goodput metric for training clusters \citep{dlbook,taxbreak2026}.


\section{Online Softmax}
\label{sec:ch10_online_softmax}
\paragraph{Step-by-step decode loop (one head).} At token $t$, the query vector $q$ is fixed. For each KV block $b=1\ldots B$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item TMA or vector loads fetch $K_b,V_b$ tiles into on-chip storage \citep{semianalysis2024tma,kwon2023vllm}.
\item MMA or dot products form score tile $s_b$ \citep{nvidia2022hopper,dao2022flashattention}.
\item Warp max-reduce finds $m_b$; threads compute $e^{s_b-m_b'}$ with updated global max $m_b'$ \citep{dao2022flashattention}.
\item Accumulate $\ell_b$ and $o_b=\sum \mathrm{softmax}(s_b)\,V_b$ \citep{dao2022flashattention,dao2023flashdecoding}.
\item Merge into running $(m,\ell,o)$ without global memory \citep{yirage2026,memoryfloor2026}.
\end{enumerate}
Finalize $o/\ell$ once after block $B$ \citep{dlbook}. This loop is the device-side spec Chapter~11 MegaKernels implement \citep{taxbreak2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}.

\paragraph{Bytes/token estimate (sketch).} Materializing $P$ adds roughly $2T\cdot d_{\mathrm{head}}$ bytes read/write per head per layer per token (scores + probabilities), ignoring cache effects \citep{memoryfloor2026,patel2025llminference}. Online softmax removes the $P$ term; remaining traffic is dominated by KV and weights \citep{kwon2023vllm,dao2022flashattention}. Use this sketch to justify fusion PRs when microbench GEMMs look unchanged \citep{taxbreak2026}.

\paragraph{Comparison to SDPA eager paths.} PyTorch scaled dot-product attention may call cuDNN or flash backends depending on shape \citep{patel2025llminference,dlbook}. At batch-1 decode on bandwidth-saturated GPUs, swapping SDPA variants without fixing launches/bytes can \emph{regress} latency \citep{memoryfloor2026,taxbreak2026}. Online softmax must be evaluated inside full-layer counters, matching Fregly's profile-first methodology \citep{patel2025llminference}.


\paragraph{The naive failure mode.} Standard attention computes scores $s_{ij}= q_i \cdot k_j / \sqrt{d_h}$, applies softmax row-wise, multiplies by $V$ \citep{dlbook,dao2022flashattention}. Materializing $P$ for decode means an $1\times T$ vector per head per layer per token---small individually, catastrophic when each block boundary exports scores or probabilities to HBM \citep{taxbreak2026,memoryfloor2026}. Online softmax avoids storing $P$ entirely \citep{dao2022flashattention}.

For block $t$ with local scores $x^{(t)}_j$, maintain running maximum $m$, sum $\ell=\sum \exp(x_j-m)$ across blocks seen so far, and accumulator $o=\sum_j \mathrm{softmax}(x)_j v_j$ \citep{dao2022flashattention}. When a new block arrives with local max $m_t$ and local sum $\ell_t=\sum_j e^{x^{(t)}_j-m_t}$, update:
\begin{align*}
m' &= \max(m, m_t), \\
\ell' &= \ell \cdot e^{m-m'} + \ell_t \cdot e^{m_t-m'}, \\
o' &= o \cdot e^{m-m'} + o_t \cdot e^{m_t-m'}.
\end{align*}
After the final block, $o/\ell$ is the attention output row \citep{dao2022flashattention,dao2023flashdecoding}. Figure~\ref{fig:online_softmax_pipeline} is the control-flow spine every fused decode kernel must implement \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}.

\paragraph{Log-sum-exp view.} Equivalently, online softmax tracks $\log \ell$ and $m$ so merges use stable log-add operations \citep{dlbook}. Compiler backends may emit either multiplicative or log-space updates; IR must preserve associativity order for deterministic mode \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}. Mixing orders across GPU warps without documentation breaks bitwise regression tests \citep{patel2025llminference}.

\paragraph{Multi-head carrier layout.} With $h$ heads, each head owns $(m_h,\ell_h,o_h)$ or shares warp groups \citep{dao2022flashattention}. MegaKernels pack carriers in shared memory with head-major or block-major layouts depending on MMA tile shapes \citep{semianalysis2024tma,nvidia2022hopper}. XDNA assigns fixed SRAM slots per head at compile time---dynamic head counts require separate compiled graphs \citep{amd2024aie,amd2024xdna}.

Table~\ref{tab:online_softmax} explains why ``online'' is a \textbf{residency contract}, not an algorithmic nicety \citep{memoryfloor2026,patel2025llminference}. GPU implementations keep $(m,\ell,o)$ in warp registers or shared memory for the duration of the KV block loop \citep{semianalysis2024tma,nvidia2022hopper}. Exporting carriers between blocks to global memory doubles traffic and breaks Chapter~11 fusion \citep{taxbreak2026,yirage2026}.

\paragraph{Paged KV interaction.} vLLM-style block tables change \emph{where} $K/V$ bytes live, not whether online updates run each block \citep{kwon2023vllm}. Device-side pointer walks replace scatter kernels but the inner softmax state machine is unchanged \citep{patel2025llminference}. Block size interacts with tile shapes: 16-token blocks may underutilize bandwidth; 256-token blocks may exceed shared-memory staging \citep{dao2023flashdecoding,semianalysis2024tma}.

\begin{example}
Decode cell (BS${=}1$, ctx${=}2048$, block${=}64$): 32 KV blocks per head. Materialized softmax writes 32 partial $P$ tiles to HBM; online softmax keeps three scalars/vectors per warp group on-chip \citep{dao2022flashattention,memoryfloor2026,taxbreak2026}.
\end{example}

\paragraph{FlashAttention and Flash-Decoding.} FlashAttention uses online softmax inside SRAM tiles for prefill-like parallelism \citep{dao2022flashattention}. Flash-Decoding splits KV across SMs while preserving online state per split, then merges splits with a second online reduction \citep{dao2023flashdecoding}. Decode MegaKernels must choose split strategy \emph{inside} one launch or pay multiplied graph steps \citep{taxbreak2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs}. Split-merge is easy to get wrong: an extra global sync per split negates Flash-Decoding wins \citep{patel2025llminference}.

\paragraph{Split-KV merge protocol.} When SM $s$ finishes block range $[b_s, b_e)$ with local $(m_s,\ell_s,o_s)$, a tree reduction combines pairs using the same merge equations until one global $(m,\ell,o)$ remains \citep{dao2023flashdecoding}. Implementations either use a dedicated merge kernel (bad for launches/token) or warp-specialized epilogue warps inside the MegaKernel (preferred) \citep{taxbreak2026,yirage2026}. Profile merge separately---it becomes visible at long context when split count grows \citep{patel2025llminference}.

\paragraph{GQA and MQA carrier savings.} Grouped-query attention shares $K/V$ across query heads, reducing KV bytes/token but not eliminating online softmax per query group \citep{liu2024deepseekv3,kwon2023vllm}. Carrier count scales with query groups, not raw head count---compiler passes must not allocate per-head carriers when groups share scores \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}.


\section{Numerical Stability}
\label{sec:ch10_numerical_stability}
\paragraph{Error propagation across merges.} Each merge is multiplicatively stable when $m$ tracks the true row max \citep{dao2022flashattention,dlbook}. If local max is underestimated, exponentials overflow and $\ell$ corrupts silently \citep{patel2025llminference}. Tests must include adversarial score spikes at random positions within long blocks \citep{dao2023flashdecoding,yirage2026}.

\paragraph{Determinism vs performance.} Parallel max reductions across warps choose associativity order \citep{nvidia2022hopper,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}. Compliance workloads may require deterministic reductions; interactive serving often tolerates bounded drift \citep{patel2025llminference,dlbook}. Document reduction order in kernel headers and YiRage lowering logs \citep{yirage2026}.

\paragraph{Interaction with rotary embeddings.} RoPE rotates $q,k$ before $QK^\top$; it does not change softmax merge math but shifts score distributions with position \citep{liu2024deepseekv3,kwon2023vllm}. Long-context stability tests must use RoPE-enabled references, not static $q,k$ \citep{dao2023flashdecoding,patel2025llminference}.


\paragraph{Why max-subtraction is non-optional.} Softmax exponentials overflow in FP16/BF16 without subtracting row max \citep{dlbook,patel2025llminference}. Online softmax \emph{is} the stable formulation---but only if each block computes local max before \textbf{exp} \citep{dao2022flashattention}. A ``fast path'' that skips max on ``small'' blocks fails at long contexts when score magnitudes drift with RoPE position \citep{dao2023flashdecoding,memoryfloor2026}.

Hopper SFUs accelerate \textbf{exp} relative to prior generations---mechanical sympathy at the silicon level for transformer attention \citep{nvidia2022hopper,patel2025llminference}.

\paragraph{FP8 and block scales.} FP8 decode experiments carry per-block or per-tensor scales into the online merge \citep{patel2025llminference,liu2024deepseekv3}. Re-quantizing scores in a separate kernel breaks fusion and often breaks stability unless scales participate in the max-find \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}. Treat scale vectors like carrier extensions in IR lifetime analysis \citep{memoryfloor2026}.

\paragraph{Precision pitfalls.} Using FP16 for $\ell$ while FP32 for $m$ is common; using FP16 for both can underflow $\ell$ at long $T$ \citep{dao2022flashattention,patel2025llminference}. BF16 often behaves better for decode carriers but still needs FP32 accumulation for $o$ \citep{dlbook,yirage2026}. Compiler passes should declare accumulator dtypes explicitly \citep{chen2020compilerbasedhardw,yirage2026}.

\paragraph{Testing stability.} Compare online vs reference double-softmax on random $q,k,v$ across $T\in\{512,2048,8192\}$ \citep{dao2023flashdecoding,kwon2023vllm}. Failures cluster at long contexts and low head dims \citep{patel2025llminference,dlbook}. Production gates require bounded-error tests before enabling fused paths in YiRage exports \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}.

\paragraph{Attention sinks and bias terms.} Some models add attention sinks or ALiBi-style biases before softmax \citep{liu2024deepseekv3}. Online updates must incorporate biases into block-local max and exp before merge---a separate bias kernel between $QK^\top$ and softmax reintroduces Boundary A breaks \citep{taxbreak2026,dao2022flashattention}.


\section{Cross-Hardware Online Softmax}
\label{sec:ch10_cross_hardware}
\paragraph{Bandwidth-normalized comparison.} Report online softmax wins as $\Delta$bytes/token and $\Delta$kernels/token, not only ms/token \citep{memoryfloor2026,taxbreak2026}. A CPU win of 5\% latency with 40\% bytes removed tells a different story than GPU ms shifts entangled with driver version \citep{patel2025llminference,dlbook}.

\paragraph{XDNA compile failures as signals.} When XDNA rejects a schedule for carrier SRAM overflow, treat it as a fusion boundary violation to fix upstream---not as ``NPU unsupported'' \citep{amd2024aie,yirage2026}. Successful GPU kernels that spill carriers are equally incorrect, just slower instead of failing compile \citep{memoryfloor2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}.

\paragraph{Edge Ryzen AI deployment.} DDR-contended edge hosts mirror CPU patterns: KV bandwidth dominates; online softmax prevents an extra $P$ pass but cannot fix unfused MLP boundaries \citep{amd2024xdna,patel2025llminference}. Edge fleets should still measure launches/token even when ``launch'' means DMA edge count \citep{amd2024aie,taxbreak2026}.


\paragraph{Same math, different residency tables.} Online softmax is target-agnostic in equations but not in memory hierarchy \citep{amd2024xdna,nvidia2022hopper,dlbook}. A schedule legal on Hopper may illegalize on XDNA when carrier slots exceed tile SRAM \citep{amd2024aie,yirage2026}. CPU decode uses SIMD registers for $(m,\ell)$ and blocked $o$ updates with cache-aware KV reads \citep{dlbook,patel2025llminference}.

\paragraph{GPU (Hopper-class).} Warp-level reductions compute block max; \textbf{exp} uses fast math intrinsics with documented error bounds \citep{nvidia2022hopper,semianalysis2024tma}. TMA loads $K/V$ blocks while MMA computes $QK^\top$ fragments \citep{semianalysis2024tma}. Carriers stay in registers owned by the softmax warpgroup---not in HBM \citep{memoryfloor2026,dao2022flashattention}. Flash-Decoding maps splits to SMs but must merge carriers without an extra launch when possible \citep{dao2023flashdecoding,taxbreak2026}.

\paragraph{XDNA / AIE.} Static dataflow assigns fixed SRAM addresses for $(m,\ell,o)$ for each pipeline stage \citep{amd2024aie,amd2024xdna}. Online updates become MAC/DMA micro-programs; there is no dynamic spill \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}. If softmax carriers cannot be placed, the graph fails compile---correct outcome \citep{amd2024aie,memoryfloor2026}. Teams porting CUDA softmax often expect runtime allocation; XDNA requires upfront bucketization by context length \citep{kwon2023vllm,amd2024xdna}.

\paragraph{CPU.} oneDNN/OpenBLAS-style paths vectorize \textbf{exp} and use FP32 accumulators \citep{dlbook}. KV bandwidth dominates; online softmax savings are real but smaller than GPU HBM round-trip elimination \citep{patel2025llminference,memoryfloor2026}. NUMA-local KV still matters for multi-socket hosts \citep{kwon2023vllm,dlbook}.

\begin{example}
Identical Llama head at ctx${=}2048$: Hopper fused online softmax keeps carriers in registers; XDNA maps them to tile slot \#3; CPU holds $m,\ell$ in AVX registers---same invariants, different compiler legality checks \citep{amd2024xdna,semianalysis2024tma,dlbook,yirage2026}.
\end{example}

\paragraph{Triplet regression.} Never benchmark GPU only and infer XDNA behavior \citep{amd2024aie,patel2025llminference}. YiRage triplet-test from one IR export should show bytes/token deltas, not just ms/token, so mechanism changes are visible across targets \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw,liu2024deepseekv3}.

\paragraph{Blackwell / next-gen SFU note.} Newer GPUs continue to widen SFU throughput for softmax-heavy decode \citep{nvidia2022hopper,patel2025llminference}. Online softmax still wins on bytes moved; faster \textbf{exp} alone does not remove materialized $P$ \citep{memoryfloor2026,dao2022flashattention}. Codesign means updating tile sizes when SFU:MA ratios shift \citep{semianalysis2024tma}.


\section{Softmax Fusion Boundaries}
\label{sec:ch10_fusion_boundary}
\paragraph{Case study narrative (qualitative).} Team A graph-captures unfused attention+MLP; Team B fuses online softmax with $PV$ and keeps attn output in shared memory through SwiGLU \citep{nvidia2024cudagraphs,taxbreak2026}. Team A improves host time; Team B improves bytes/token \citep{memoryfloor2026,patel2025llminference}. Both claim ``fusion''---only Team B advances mechanical sympathy \citep{dao2022flashattention,yirage2026}.

\paragraph{Autotuner guardrails.} Search algorithms that reward FLOPs or occupancy may break Boundary B \citep{chen2020compilerbasedhardw,yirage2026}. Hard-constrain IR: no global stores on carrier SSA values; penalize $P$ materialization with infinite cost \citep{memoryfloor2026,taxbreak2026}.

\paragraph{Serving engine integration.} vLLM and TensorRT-LLM integrate flash-style attention but expose different fusion depth to custom compilers \citep{kwon2023vllm,patel2025llminference}. When exporting graphs to YiRage, preserve online regions through Boundaries A--B or explicitly document breaks for regression tests \citep{yirage2026,liu2024deepseekv3}.


\paragraph{Where online softmax ends.} The \textbf{fuse} decision is not ``softmax kernel + matmul kernel.'' It is: do $(m,\ell,o)$ and the $PV$ accumulator survive into the $O$ projection and MLP without HBM export \citep{taxbreak2026,memoryfloor2026,dao2022flashattention}? Chapter~11's MegaKernel assumes the answer is yes \citep{taxbreak2026,yirage2026}. Breaking fusion here forces separate launches and full-width tensors \citep{nvidia2024cudagraphs,kwon2023vllm}.

\paragraph{Boundary A: after $QK^\top$, before $PV$.} Fusing \textbf{online} softmax with $PV$ inside the KV loop is the FlashAttention core \citep{dao2022flashattention}. Splitting materialized $P$ to HBM between them negates IO savings \citep{memoryfloor2026}.

\paragraph{Boundary B: after attention output, before MLP.} Even perfect online softmax inside attention fails if attn output is written to HBM before SwiGLU \citep{taxbreak2026,patel2025llminference}. This is the dominant MegaKernel seam \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw,liu2024deepseekv3}.

\paragraph{Boundary C: debug stats export.} Prefill microbenchmarks sometimes keep softmax stats for debugging---strip them in inference exports \citep{dlbook,nvidia2024cudagraphs}. Accidental stat tensors reintroduce $O(T)$ HBM traffic \citep{memoryfloor2026}.

Table~\ref{tab:fusion_boundary} ties MegaKernel planning to TaxBreak counters \citep{taxbreak2026,memoryfloor2026}. Compiler \textbf{fuse} passes must label preserved boundaries in IR metadata \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}. Autotuners must not ``win'' on FLOPs by breaking Boundary B \citep{patel2025llminference,semianalysis2024tma}.

\paragraph{Worked contrast.} Hopper may keep carriers in registers through Boundary B with sufficient shared memory \citep{semianalysis2024tma,nvidia2022hopper}. XDNA requires static slot assignment for the same lifetime \citep{amd2024aie}. CPU may split Boundary B if L2 is exceeded---document the split explicitly in fleet configs \citep{dlbook,yirage2026}.

\paragraph{CUDA Graphs without residency.} Memory-Floor shows CUDA Graphs on Qwen-2.5-7B yield $1.259\times$ on H100 by removing host overhead yet leave HBM traffic unchanged \citep{memoryfloor2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs}. Graph-capturing an unfused softmax+$PV$ pair reduces launches but not Boundary A bytes---the Fregly-style distinction between orchestration relief and mechanical sympathy \citep{taxbreak2026,patel2025llminference}.

\paragraph{IR metadata contract.} YiRage should record \texttt{online\_softmax\_carriers} lifetime through Boundaries A--B so backends reject schedules that insert \texttt{GlobalStore} on $P$ or attn output \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}. Code review diffs should show eliminated stores, not just fused kernel names \citep{memoryfloor2026,taxbreak2026}.



\paragraph{Industry context: DeepSeek MLA and Flash paths.} DeepSeek's Multi-Head Latent Attention compresses KV and reshapes attention math for bandwidth-limited H800 deployments \citep{liu2024deepseekv3,patel2025llminference}. Whether scores pass through a latent projection or full $QK^\top$, the softmax step still demands online carriers when decode avoids materialized $P$ \citep{dao2022flashattention}. Treat MLA as a different $QK$ producer feeding the same state machine in Figure~\ref{fig:online_softmax_pipeline}---not an excuse to skip online updates \citep{yirage2026,taxbreak2026}.

\paragraph{Kernel launch accounting.} Separating ``stable exp'' into its own kernel adds one launch per KV block in the worst case---multiplied by layer count and token steps \citep{taxbreak2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs}. At 847 launches/token baseline for dense Llama-3.2-1B, even +1 launch/layer/block is measurable in p99 \citep{taxbreak2026,memoryfloor2026}. Online softmax belongs inside the same captured body as $QK^\top$ and $PV$ \citep{dao2023flashdecoding,yirage2026}.

\paragraph{Compiler lowering sketch.} YiRage-style IR represents \texttt{OnlineSoftmax} as a region with explicit carrier SSA values live across \texttt{For} loops over KV blocks \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}. GPU lowering maps carriers to registers; XDNA lowering maps them to tile addresses; CPU lowering maps them to vector registers with spill forbidden by legality check \citep{amd2024aie,dlbook}. Any pass that inserts \texttt{GlobalStore} on carriers must fail closed \citep{memoryfloor2026}.

\paragraph{Warp-level implementation notes.} A common GPU pattern assigns one warp group to max-reduce scores, another to scaled \textbf{exp}, and a third to accumulate $o_t$ against $V$ fragments \citep{semianalysis2024tma,nvidia2022hopper}. Shared-memory staging holds the current score tile; carriers live in registers owned by the softmax leader lane \citep{dao2022flashattention}. Bank conflicts on score tiles show up as p99 spikes at certain head dims---profile before declaring softmax ``done'' \citep{patel2025llminference,taxbreak2026}.

\paragraph{Roofline for softmax epilogue.} Even when $QK^\top$ is bandwidth-bound, materializing $P$ adds an extra read/write pass over $T$ elements per head \citep{memoryfloor2026,dlbook}. Online softmax removes that pass; the remaining work is dominated by $PV$ and KV streaming \citep{dao2022flashattention,dao2023flashdecoding}. Optimizing \textbf{exp} alone without removing $P$ is the attention analog of CUDA Graphs without residency relief \citep{memoryfloor2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs}.

\paragraph{Long-context regression matrix.} Stability tests should include: (i) random Gaussian $q,k$; (ii) structured spikes simulating attention sinks; (iii) FP8 with scales \citep{liu2024deepseekv3,patel2025llminference}. Report max absolute error vs FP64 reference at $T=8192$ and document dtype choices in Table~\ref{tab:stability_dtypes} \citep{dao2023flashdecoding,yirage2026}. Fleet releases without this matrix risk silent quality drift in fused paths \citep{chen2020compilerbasedhardw}.

\paragraph{Disaggregated prefill/decode.} When decode nodes run steady batch-1 steps, online softmax runs every token against a growing KV cache \citep{patel2025llminference,kwon2023vllm}. Prefill nodes may use different tile shapes; do not reuse prefill-only softmax kernels on decode pools without carrier residency review \citep{dao2023flashdecoding,taxbreak2026}. Disaggregation shifts optimization target toward per-step latency and in-kernel persistence \citep{memoryfloor2026}.

\paragraph{Expert/MoE caveat.} MoE decode adds routing launches unrelated to softmax---TaxBreak shows 9,305 launches/token for OLMoE-class models \citep{taxbreak2026}. Online softmax still matters inside attention, but fleet diagnostics must separate routing tax from attention residency tax \citep{patel2025llminference,liu2024deepseekv3}. Do not attribute MoE launch storms to softmax materialization without profiling \citep{taxbreak2026}.

\paragraph{Tooling: what to profile.} Use Nsight Compute warp stall reasons on softmax regions; PyTorch profiler op boundaries to count separate \texttt{softmax} ops; IR diff to verify carrier lifetimes \citep{patel2025llminference,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}. Goodput improvement shows up as reduced DRAM bytes and fewer \texttt{softmax}/\texttt{exp} kernels---not higher tensor-core utilization \citep{memoryfloor2026,taxbreak2026}.

\paragraph{Anti-pattern: ``FlashAttention enabled'' checkbox.} Enabling FlashAttention in a framework server removes many prefill pitfalls but may leave decode MLP/norm boundaries unfused \citep{dao2022flashattention,dao2023flashdecoding}. Online softmax inside flash kernels is necessary, not sufficient, for Chapter~11 MegaKernels \citep{taxbreak2026,yirage2026}. Benchmark bytes/token across the full decoder layer, not attention isolation \citep{memoryfloor2026,patel2025llminference}.

\paragraph{Handoff checklist to Chapter~11.} Before merging MegaKernel work, verify: (1) carriers never stored globally between KV blocks; (2) Boundary B preserved in IR; (3) split-KV merge in same launch; (4) dtype/stability tests pass at max context bucket \citep{yirage2026,amd2024aie,semianalysis2024tma}. Failure at any item means Chapter~11 fusion is cosmetic graph capture \citep{memoryfloor2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs}.




\paragraph{Quantitative anchor (TaxBreak).} Dense Llama-3.2-1B at BS${=}4$/SL${=}2048$ averages 847.5 GPU kernel launches per \emph{output} token on H100 \citep{taxbreak2026}. Attention softmax epilogues are a fraction of those launches, but materializing $P$ adds sync points that block fusion with $PV$ and MLP \citep{memoryfloor2026,dao2022flashattention}. Removing $P$ is prerequisite to collapsing launches toward O(1) per layer---the Chapter~11 target \citep{taxbreak2026,yirage2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs}.

\paragraph{Quantitative anchor (Memory-Floor).} CUDA Graphs on Qwen-2.5-7B (BS${=}1$, ctx${=}2048$) yields $1.259\times$ on H100 by removing ${\approx}3.05$\,ms host overhead yet leaves device bytes unchanged \citep{memoryfloor2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs}. Online softmax addresses device bytes; graphs address host launches---compose both, do not confuse either with MegaKernel residency \citep{taxbreak2026,patel2025llminference}.

\paragraph{Software stack placement.} Online softmax sits below framework servers and above MMA/TMA microkernels \citep{kwon2023vllm,semianalysis2024tma}. Compilers own carrier lifetimes; hand-written CUDA owns tile sizes \citep{chen2020compilerbasedhardw,yirage2026}. Performance engineers own regression matrices tying both to ms/token and goodput \citep{patel2025llminference,taxbreak2026}.

\paragraph{Reader exercise (mental model).} For ctx${=}4096$, block${=}128$, estimate KV blocks per head, carrier count per warp group, and whether materialized $P$ adds one or two full DRAM passes \citep{dao2022flashattention,memoryfloor2026}. If you cannot estimate bytes/token on a whiteboard, profiler tuning will misfire \citep{patel2025llminference,dlbook}.

\paragraph{Safety and monitoring.} Production fleets should log fused-path flags and context buckets per request \citep{kwon2023vllm,patel2025llminference}. Silent fallback to eager softmax when fusion legality fails must alarm---otherwise SLO regressions hide behind rare code paths \citep{yirage2026,memoryfloor2026,taxbreak2026}.

\paragraph{Cross-chapter dependencies.} Chapter~8 TMA double-buffering feeds KV tiles into this state machine \citep{semianalysis2024tma}. Chapter~9 sync hierarchy ensures carrier merges see consistent $V$ fragments \citep{amd2024aie,dao2023flashdecoding}. Chapter~11 assumes this chapter's invariants when fusing MLP \citep{taxbreak2026,yirage2026}. Skipping any prerequisite yields ``MegaKernels'' that are launch wrappers \citep{memoryfloor2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs}.

\paragraph{Glossary alignment.} \textbf{Online softmax} = streaming softmax with $(m,\ell,o)$ carriers; \textbf{materialized softmax} = writing $P$ to memory; \textbf{Boundary A/B} = fusion seams defined in Section~\ref{sec:ch10_fusion_boundary} \citep{dao2022flashattention,taxbreak2026}. Use these terms consistently in design docs and IR comments \citep{chen2020compilerbasedhardw,yirage2026}.

\paragraph{Cost framing.} At cloud scale, wasted HBM bytes translate directly to rented GPU seconds \citep{patel2025llminference,liu2024deepseekv3}. Online softmax is a cost optimization---similar to Fregly's goodput ROI argument for performance engineers \citep{taxbreak2026,dlbook}. Small per-token byte savings compound across millions of tokens and hundreds of layers \citep{memoryfloor2026,kwon2023vllm}.

\paragraph{When online softmax is insufficient.} If MLP and norm boundaries remain unfused, fixing softmax alone cannot hit aggressive ms/token targets \citep{taxbreak2026,patel2025llminference}. Treat this chapter as necessary infrastructure, not the final decode optimization \citep{yirage2026,dao2023flashdecoding}. The profile-driven engineer sequences work: residency first, launches second, FLOPs last \citep{memoryfloor2026,semianalysis2024tma}.

\paragraph{Documentation deliverables.} Ship with each fused kernel: pseudocode of merge equations, dtype table, max context bucket, split-KV merge diagram, and triplet bytes/token table \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw,amd2024xdna}. This is the O'Reilly-style ``tooling + methodology'' pairing applied to compiler outputs \citep{patel2025llminference,taxbreak2026}.

\section{Profiling Online Softmax in Production Stacks}
\label{sec:ch10_profiling}
\paragraph{Latency composition reminder.} Per-token decode latency aggregates weight read, KV read, attention math, MLP, sampling, and host orchestration \citep{taxbreak2026,patel2025llminference,kwon2023vllm}. Online softmax attacks the attention math \emph{memory} term and enables fusion that reduces orchestration \citep{dao2022flashattention,memoryfloor2026,yirage2026}. Expect partial ms/token impact if MLP boundaries remain unfused---consistent with Memory-Floor graph results \citep{memoryfloor2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs}.

\paragraph{Book roadmap hook.} Part~V manual MegaKernels assume you can hold $(m,\ell,o)$ through Boundaries A--B \citep{taxbreak2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}. Part~VI YiRage passes automate carrier lifetime analysis \citep{yirage2026,amd2024aie}. This chapter is the semantic foundation both parts reference \citep{semianalysis2024tma,dao2023flashdecoding,patel2025llminference,dlbook,liu2024deepseekv3,memoryfloor2026,kwon2023vllm,nvidia2024cudagraphs}.


\paragraph{Profile-first workflow (Fregly-aligned).} Start from user-visible ms/token at batch-1, then decompose with TaxBreak-style launch accounting and Memory-Floor-style bandwidth floors \citep{taxbreak2026,memoryfloor2026}. If $R_{\mathrm{floor}}$ is low on H100, prioritize eliminating $P$ materialization and Boundary B breaks before tuning \textbf{exp} microkernels \citep{memoryfloor2026,patel2025llminference}. If $R_{\mathrm{floor}}$ is high on L4, bytes/token from materialized softmax may still dominate---online carriers remain mandatory \citep{memoryfloor2026,dao2022flashattention}.

\paragraph{Nsight Compute signals.} High \texttt{memory\_throughput} with low \texttt{sm\_\_throughput} during softmax regions suggests $P$ traffic or spill \citep{patel2025llminference,semianalysis2024tma}. High \texttt{warp\_stall\_math} on \textbf{exp} may justify SFU tuning \emph{after} residency is fixed \citep{nvidia2022hopper}. Treat profiler screenshots as release artifacts tied to IR hashes \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}.

\paragraph{Framework-level visibility.} PyTorch profiler timelines that show distinct \texttt{softmax} ops per layer per token indicate Boundary A breaks in eager stacks \citep{dlbook,taxbreak2026}. torch.compile may fuse some epilogues but cannot invent on-chip carriers if the graph exports $P$ \citep{chen2020compilerbasedhardw,patel2025llminference}. Compare against a hand-written online reference kernel on identical shapes before trusting compiler fusion \citep{dao2022flashattention,yirage2026}.

\paragraph{Regression gates for YiRage exports.} Every backend export should attach: (i) bytes/token delta vs eager; (ii) kernels/token delta; (iii) max abs error vs FP64 at $T=8192$; (iv) legality pass/fail on XDNA \citep{yirage2026,amd2024aie,memoryfloor2026}. This mirrors MLPerf-style reproducibility discipline adapted to compiler outputs \citep{patel2025llminference,taxbreak2026}.

\paragraph{Teaching vs shipping paths.} Educational notebooks often materialize $P$ for clarity \citep{dlbook}. Production IR must strip those tensors---accidental debug hooks reintroduce HBM traffic at scale \citep{memoryfloor2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs}. Separate ``debug softmax stats'' behind compile flags, default off \citep{chen2020compilerbasedhardw,yirage2026}.

\paragraph{Historical note.} Online softmax predates LLM serving---streaming softmax appears in numerical libraries and early attention approximations \citep{dlbook,dao2022flashattention}. FlashAttention's contribution is pairing online updates with IO-aware tiling on GPUs \citep{dao2022flashattention,dao2023flashdecoding}. This chapter's decode focus follows industrial shift toward batch-1 latency and MegaKernel fusion \citep{taxbreak2026,kwon2023vllm,patel2025llminference}.

\paragraph{Open problems.} Extremely long contexts ($T>128$K) stress $\ell$ dynamic range even in FP32 \citep{patel2025llminference,liu2024deepseekv3}. Block-wise normalization tricks and periodic rescaling may enter carrier state---document any non-standard invariant before Chapter~11 assumes three scalars suffice \citep{yirage2026,dao2023flashdecoding}. Research prototypes belong behind explicit IR ops, not silent kernel hacks \citep{chen2020compilerbasedhardw}.


\paragraph{Summary before takeaways.} Sections~\ref{sec:ch10_online_softmax}--\ref{sec:ch10_fusion_boundary} form a single story: streaming softmax math, stable numerics, target-specific residency, and fusion seams measured by TaxBreak and Memory-Floor counters \citep{taxbreak2026,memoryfloor2026,dao2022flashattention,amd2024xdna,yirage2026}. Section~\ref{sec:ch10_profiling} ties those threads to profiler workflows familiar from O\'Reilly-style systems books---start from user latency, decompose bottlenecks, verify bytes and launches, then tune kernels \citep{patel2025llminference,semianalysis2024tma,chen2020compilerbasedhardw,dlbook,kwon2023vllm,liu2024deepseekv3,nvidia2024cudagraphs}.
\section{Key Takeaways}
\label{sec:ch10_key_takeaways}


\paragraph{Appendix cross-reference.} Verified launch and bandwidth numbers for this chapter appear in Chapter~1 Table~\ref{tab:verified_snapshot} and Appendix regression matrices \citep{taxbreak2026,memoryfloor2026,patel2025llminference}. When citing ms/token improvements here, always pair with kernels/token and bytes/token from the same run configuration \citep{yirage2026,dlbook}. Mixed sources invalidate cross-chapter comparisons \citep{chen2020compilerbasedhardw}.

\paragraph{Speculative decoding interaction.} Speculative paths run draft models with additional forward passes \citep{patel2025llminference,liu2024deepseekv3}. Online softmax in the target model remains unchanged in math; draft acceptance does not remove KV loop residency requirements \citep{dao2022flashattention,kwon2023vllm}. Profile speculative stacks separately---draft overhead dominates some deployments before attention bytes do \citep{taxbreak2026,memoryfloor2026}.

\paragraph{Energy angle.} HBM reads dominate decode energy \citep{memoryfloor2026,patel2025llminference}. Online softmax reduces unnecessary DRAM toggling---relevant for edge Ryzen AI and cloud TCO even when ms/token gains look modest \citep{amd2024xdna,amd2024aie,dlbook}. Energy-aware fleets should log bytes/token alongside power counters \citep{yirage2026,taxbreak2026}.

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Stream, do not materialize.} Maintain the running statistics $(m,\ell,o)$ across KV blocks so the softmax probability matrix $P$ is never written to HBM during decode \citep{dao2022flashattention,memoryfloor2026}. Materializing $P$ even as a thin $1\times T$ vector per head per token is catastrophic once block boundaries export it, which is exactly the traffic online softmax removes \citep{taxbreak2026,kwon2023vllm}.
\item \textbf{Max before \textbf{exp}.} Stability is part of performance---unstable online paths force FP32 fallbacks that break fusion \citep{dao2022flashattention,patel2025llminference}.
\item \textbf{Measure attention goodput.} Track bytes/token and launches/token alongside ms/token; FLOPs mis-rank bottlenecks at batch-1 \citep{taxbreak2026,dlbook}.
\item \textbf{Mechanical sympathy across targets.} GPU registers, XDNA SRAM slots, and CPU SIMD accumulators implement the same invariants with different legality rules \citep{amd2024xdna,semianalysis2024tma,dlbook}.
\item \textbf{Respect fusion boundaries.} Boundaries A--B determine MegaKernel legality; graphs alone do not fix Boundary breaks \citep{memoryfloor2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs,yirage2026}.
\item \textbf{Split-KV needs in-kernel merge.} Flash-Decoding wins require second-stage online reduction without extra launches \citep{dao2023flashdecoding,taxbreak2026}.
\item \textbf{Profile-driven gates.} Long-context numerical tests and buffer accounting block merges---not microbench GEMM tweaks \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}.

\item \textbf{Pair the metrics, as Fregly pairs goodput with utilization.} Always report kernels/token and bytes/token alongside ms/token on the same chart, because either number alone hides the other limiter at batch-1 \citep{taxbreak2026,memoryfloor2026}. A softmax change that improves one while quietly regressing the other is invisible unless the metrics are shown together \citep{patel2025llminference,dlbook}.
\item \textbf{Reject graph-only wins.} Orchestration speedups without residency relief are partial fixes---label them explicitly in benchmark tables \citep{nvidia2024cudagraphs,memoryfloor2026}.
\item \textbf{Document dtype and context buckets.} Fleet configs must pin max context, block size, and carrier dtypes per compiled graph \citep{kwon2023vllm,yirage2026,amd2024aie}.
\end{itemize}



\paragraph{Closing integration note.} Industrial decode optimization is a stack problem: online softmax fixes attention residency, MegaKernels fix layer residency, disaggregation fixes fleet residency \citep{patel2025llminference,kwon2023vllm,taxbreak2026}. None replaces the others \citep{memoryfloor2026,liu2024deepseekv3}. Performance engineers document which layer of the stack each PR addresses---avoid claiming end-to-end wins from softmax alone \citep{yirage2026,dlbook}.

\paragraph{Vendor roadmap awareness.} Faster SFUs, TMA, and FP4 tensor cores change \emph{how} softmax is implemented, not \emph{whether} carriers stay on-chip \citep{nvidia2022hopper,semianalysis2024tma,patel2025llminference}. Re-tile kernels each silicon generation; invariants in this chapter remain \citep{dao2022flashattention,chen2020compilerbasedhardw}. Mechanical sympathy evolves with hardware but rejects materialized $P$ in decode \citep{memoryfloor2026,taxbreak2026}.

\paragraph{Collaboration boundaries.} Model teams own architecture; systems teams own serving; compiler teams own IR legality \citep{yirage2026,patel2025llminference}. Online softmax sits at the intersection---requires joint review when changing head dims, context limits, or dtypes \citep{liu2024deepseekv3,kwon2023vllm}. Fregly's cross-team transparency principle applies: publish benchmark configs alongside kernel changes \citep{taxbreak2026,memoryfloor2026}.

\paragraph{Final sanity check.} If your fused attention kernel writes a tensor named \texttt{attn\_weights} or \texttt{probs} to global memory during decode, you are not running online softmax in the production sense \citep{dao2022flashattention,memoryfloor2026}. Rename, refactor, or reject the merge \citep{yirage2026,chen2020compilerbasedhardw,taxbreak2026}.


\paragraph{Execution checklist.} Before closing a softmax fusion PR: confirm IR shows no \texttt{GlobalStore} on probabilities; run FP64 reference at $T=8192$; capture Nsight DRAM throughput delta; re-run TaxBreak launches/token on BS${=}1$; validate XDNA compile legality or document intentional CPU/GPU-only scope \citep{yirage2026,taxbreak2026,memoryfloor2026,amd2024aie,patel2025llminference,dao2022flashattention,chen2020compilerbasedhardw,semianalysis2024tma,dlbook,kwon2023vllm,nvidia2024cudagraphs,liu2024deepseekv3,dao2023flashdecoding}.
\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:ch10_conclusion}

Online softmax is the decode attention state machine: streaming KV blocks update $(m,\ell,o)$ without materializing $P$ \citep{dao2022flashattention,dao2023flashdecoding}. Numerical discipline and multi-hardware residency tables determine whether compiler-generated schedules can fuse through Boundaries A and B \citep{amd2024aie,yirage2026,semianalysis2024tma}. This is mechanical sympathy at attention granularity---the same design philosophy Fregly applies at cluster scale, translated into on-chip carrier lifetimes and TaxBreak-visible launch counts \citep{taxbreak2026,patel2025llminference,liu2024deepseekv3}.

Chapter~11 consumes this chapter wholesale: full-layer MegaKernels assume carriers and partial $PV$ survive into MLP fusion \citep{taxbreak2026,yirage2026}. If softmax spills here, no downstream graph capture recovers the bytes/token budget---the same way cluster-scale goodput cannot recover from idle GPUs masked by headline utilization \citep{memoryfloor2026,nvidia2024cudagraphs}. Teams that skip online carriers often chase speculative decoding or wider GPUs first---reasonable for product roadmaps, but ineffective when bytes/token and launches/token remain bound by materialized softmax and Boundary B breaks \citep{patel2025llminference,taxbreak2026,memoryfloor2026}. The disciplined sequence matches this book and Fregly\'s profile-first ethos: measure useful work, fix residency, then amortize launches, then tune FLOPs \citep{dao2022flashattention,semianalysis2024tma,dlbook}. Carry forward explicit IR boundary metadata, profiler screenshots, and triplet bytes/token tables in every release notes bundle, triplet regression across GPU/CPU/NPU, and long-context stability tests as non-negotiable release gates for any attention fusion touching decode paths \citep{chen2020compilerbasedhardw,kwon2023vllm}.

\paragraph{Handoff.} Online softmax carriers are the Part~IV residency prerequisite for full-layer MegaKernel integration in Chapter~11 on GPU, CPU, and XDNA fleet targets alike across the book. \citep{taxbreak2026,yirage2026,dao2022flashattention,memoryfloor2026,patel2025llminference,chen2020compilerbasedhardw,semianalysis2024tma,amd2024aie,dlbook,kwon2023vllm,nvidia2024cudagraphs,liu2024deepseekv3,dao2023flashdecoding}.
